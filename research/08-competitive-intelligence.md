## 1. Andy Elliott / The Elliott Group

### Daily Protocol Delivered
- **Format:** Video-based sales training modules (short-form), script practice, and role-play exercises
- **Content:** Phone sales mastery, internet lead conversion, specific closing scripts, mindset training
- **Accountability System:** Primarily community-based via Skool group; no structured daily check-in system reported
- **Daily Tasks:** Watch training videos, practice scripts, apply techniques on calls — self-directed rather than app-driven
- **Time Investment:** 30-60 minutes/day for video consumption; implementation time varies

### Weekly Cadence
- Community posts and engagement in Skool group
- No structured weekly live coaching calls reported at base tier
- Higher-tier programs include group coaching sessions

### Pricing & Perceived Value
- **Entry Level:** $299/month Skool membership
- **Mid-Tier:** "Master Closer University" at $7,500+
- **High-Ticket:** $20,000+ for personal access/mentorship
- **Pricing Framing:** Markets as "$3-5/day" for the entry membership — classic micro-cost framing
- **Perceived Value:** Deeply divided — supporters credit career transformation; critics call it "overpriced, old-school tactics"

### What Users Love
- "Andy's stuff really works if you apply it — my numbers went way up"
- Specific, actionable scripts that can be used immediately
- High-energy, motivational delivery style
- Has trained 325,000+ salespeople — real track record in automotive sales
- "No one disputes the dude can close"

### What Users Hate / Complain About
- "Overpriced, old-school tactics" for modern sales environments
- Aggressive upselling from $299/month membership into $7,500-$20,000 programs
- "$20,000 to literally just meet him — he's grifted y'all through content"
- Polarizing personality — high-energy style feels "salesy" to some
- Legal/ethical concerns: admitted under oath to crimes spanning a decade (until 2019)
- BBB: Not accredited; F rating for The Elliott Group
- "The marketing relies on unverifiable superlatives and carefully curated success stories while obscuring pricing for premium programs"
- Operates in an "unregulated industry not monitored for fraud, lies and deceit"

### Retention Signals
- **High churn indicators:** Heavy reliance on upselling suggests base membership alone doesn't retain
- **Stickiness factor:** Content is consumable (videos) not behavioral (daily system), creating completion-and-leave pattern
- **No structured accountability tracking** = lower daily engagement = higher churn
- Community engagement in Skool is the primary retention mechanism

### GAP: What's Missing
- **No structured daily operating system** — it's training content, not an accountability protocol
- **No progress tracking or scoring** — users self-report results
- **No integration with actual business metrics** — purely educational, not operational
- **No personalized protocol** — same videos for everyone regardless of business stage
- **No assessment-driven customization** — one-size-fits-all content library

## 2. Alex Hormozi / Skool / Acquisition.com

### Daily Protocol Delivered
- **Gym Launch (for gym owners):** Systematic business consulting — daily call requirements, lead generation protocols, specific outreach numbers
- **"100 Calls a Day" Methodology:** Literal prescription — make 100 outbound sales calls per day as the core growth lever
- **$100M Offers Framework:** Step-by-step workbook exercises (not a daily protocol per se — it's a strategic framework)
  - Define dream outcome in one specific sentence
  - Document signature methodology with a memorable name
  - Design three strategic bonuses solving adjacent client problems
  - Value equation: maximize Dream Outcome + Perceived Likelihood; minimize Time Delay + Effort/Sacrifice
- **Daily Practice:** Framework is "a series of exercises you need to work through step by step" — clients who do the work "generally double their revenue within 90 days"
- **Time Investment:** High — the "100 calls" methodology alone is 4-6 hours/day

### Weekly Cadence
- Skool community engagement (daily posting, commenting, challenges)
- Group coaching calls (varies by program tier)
- Community leaderboards and gamification built into Skool platform
- Weekly wins/accountability posts encouraged

### Pricing & Perceived Value
- **Free Content:** Books ($29), YouTube, podcasts — widely praised as genuinely valuable
- **Skool Communities:** Typically $97-$300/month for Hormozi-adjacent communities
- **Workshops:** $35,000-$45,000 for in-person Acquisition.com workshops
- **High-Ticket:** $5K-$50K+ for direct coaching/consulting
- **Perceived Value:** One workshop attendee called it "an absolute home run" with multiple insights worth the $35K individually
- Critics note: "His systems often assume you've already got traction and revenue" — less suitable for beginners

### What Users Love
- Free content alone scaled one entrepreneur to $500K ARR
- "Hyper tactical information and ways of thinking that help reach the next level"
- Practical, no-BS approach with specific frameworks
- Strong community in Skool (gamification drives engagement)
- Books are universally praised as high-value-per-dollar

### What Users Hate / Complain About
- "Leans heavily on hype marketing, especially with $100M messaging"
- "Sounds like other gurus who promise big numbers"
- Entry seems low-cost but upsells hit $5K-$50K+
- "Don't expect personal coaching unless you pay for workshops or services"
- Programs assume existing traction — beginners feel left behind
- Gym Launch is B2B (for gym owners) — not a daily personal accountability system

### Retention / Churn Data (Skool Platform)
- **Average Skool community churn rate: 18%** (across all Skool groups, not just Hormozi's)
- **"Good" churn rate: <10%** on Skool
- Some creators start at 30% churn, optimize down to 8%
- **Hormozi's 14 anti-churn strategies** are widely shared across Skool communities
- Main churn causes: lack of perceived value, absence of ongoing engagement, complicated UX, insufficient community building
- **Challenge participation** significantly reduces churn and increases LTV

### GAP: What's Missing
- **No personalized daily protocol** — frameworks require self-implementation
- **No automated accountability tracking** — relies on community peer pressure
- **No assessment layer** — no diagnostic to determine which framework applies to your business
- **No integrated scoring/progress system** — community engagement is the proxy for progress
- **The "$100M Offers" workbook is a one-time strategic exercise**, not a daily operating system
- **No daily operational rhythm prescribed** beyond "make more calls"

## 4. EOS Implementers (EOS Worldwide)

### Daily Protocol Delivered
- **Daily Huddle:** 5-15 minute stand-up meeting
  - Format: Share good news, review scorecard numbers, identify issues
  - Designed for leadership teams (not solo CEOs)
  - Quick pulse-check on company health

### Weekly Protocol: The Level 10 (L10) Meeting
- **Duration:** 90 minutes, same day/time every week
- **Structured Agenda:**
  1. Segue (5 min): One personal win, one professional win
  2. Scorecard Review (5 min): Leading indicators on the table
  3. Rock Review (5 min): On-track/off-track status
  4. Customer/Employee Headlines (5 min)
  5. To-Do List Review (5 min): Last week's to-dos — done or not done
  6. IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve) (60 min): The heart of the meeting
  7. Conclude (5 min): Recap to-dos, rate meeting 1-10
- **This is the single most praised element of EOS**

### Quarterly Protocol: Rocks (90-Day Goals)
- Set 3-7 "Rocks" (major priorities) per quarter
- Each leadership team member owns specific Rocks
- Quarterly pulsing creates 90-day sprints
- Annual planning session feeds into quarterly Rocks

### Pricing for Implementation
- **Self-Implementation (DIY from book):** Cost of Traction book (~$20) — but most report this is insufficient
- **Professional EOS Implementer:** $50,000-$150,000+ over 2 years
- **Typical engagement:** Monthly or bi-monthly full-day sessions with implementer
- **Full implementation timeline:** 18-24 months to master all tools

### Results & Success Statistics
- 83% of companies report increased clarity and focus
- 76% report improved team communication
- Revenue growth rates increase 18-30% for full implementers
- Profit margins improve 2-5 percentage points
- 200,000+ companies have adopted EOS worldwide
- Average revenue growth: 18% over three years

### What Users Love
- "Revenue climbed while nights-and-weekends work shrank"
- L10 meetings praised as the single most transformative tool
- Scorecard creates data-driven decision making
- Rocks create quarterly focus and accountability
- Simple, practical framework — "practical ways to apply them in a brand"
- IDS process resolves issues permanently vs. recurring discussions

### What Users Hate / Complain About
- **"For every business that successfully adopts EOS, there are two who tried but abandoned it"**
- "Level 10 meetings are a massive waste of time... hours in meetings every week"
- "Can feel cold or too corporate" — teams valuing creativity resist the structure
- "Leaders spend more time 'doing EOS' than solving real problems"
- System introduces its own jargon, "rebranding common terms"
- "EOS insists on strict adherence — deviation is seen as failure"
- "Once your company grows past ~100 employees, the system may not scale well"
- "Too many people treat 80% as the finish line" — Rocks go unfinished
- The book alone is insufficient for implementation — "stands alone quite poorly"
- Requires expensive implementer for most businesses

### Retention / Adoption Signals
- **Critical stat: "For every business that successfully adopts EOS, there are two who tried but abandoned it"** — roughly 33% success rate
- Full implementation takes 18-24 months — many quit before completion
- Consistency within leadership team typically achieved in 90 days
- Department rollout adds 6-12 months
- **Discipline fatigue** is the #1 reason businesses abandon EOS

### GAP: What's Missing
- **No technology platform** — EOS is a methodology, not software (third-party tools like Ninety.io fill this gap)
- **Designed for teams of 10-250, not solo entrepreneurs or small teams**
- **No personal development integration** — purely operational
- **No daily individual protocol** — daily huddle is a team event, not personal accountability
- **No assessment layer beyond the "Organizational Checkup"** — no ongoing diagnostics
- **Expensive to implement properly** — $50K-$150K with an implementer
- **No AI/automation** — entirely human-facilitated
- **No financial intelligence integration** — uses scorecards but doesn't connect to actual financial data

## 5. Cardone University / 10X Business Programs

### Daily Protocol Delivered
- **Video Training Modules:** 800+ short video courses covering sales and business
- **The "10X Rule" Applied Daily:**
  - Set goals 10X bigger than you think necessary
  - Take 10X more action than you think is required
  - Translation: Make more calls, send more emails, do more outreach — volume over finesse
- **Daily Activities:** Watch training videos, practice scripts, implement on calls
- **No structured daily check-in system** — self-directed video consumption
- **Time Investment:** Varies — video watching is passive; implementation is unstructured

### Weekly Cadence
- Weekly mastermind groups (at higher tiers)
- Support calls available
- Community engagement
- No structured weekly rhythm comparable to EOS L10s

### Pricing & Perceived Value
- **Entry:** Free 14-day trial, then $97/month (or $59/month promo)
- **Annual:** 12 payments of $449 (~$5,388/year)
- **Lifetime:** $24,997
- **Real Estate Club:** $25,000/year or 6x$5,000 payments
- **10X Business Coach Program:** Licensing to use Cardone's curriculum — high-ticket
- **Perceived Value:** "At $97/month for 800+ videos, pure content volume seems reasonable" — but "when factoring in quality concerns, organizational issues, and aggressive upselling, the value proposition crumbles"

### What Users Love
- "Trained from 2020-2024, boosted sales extremely, dominated sector and area"
- "No bait and switch — teaches exactly what makes money and works for him"
- Rational, applicable advice in short video modules
- Real solutions for objections, prospecting, and follow-up

### What Users Hate / Complain About

- Aggressive cold-calling of businesses to sell the program

### Retention Signals
- **Heavy content library = binge-and-leave pattern** — no daily protocol to maintain engagement
- Billing complaints suggest many try to cancel and can't

- Community engagement is secondary to content consumption

### GAP: What's Missing
- **No structured daily operating system** — it's a video library, not an accountability protocol
- **No progress tracking or scoring** — consumption metrics only
- **No assessment or personalization** — same 800 videos for everyone
- **No business metrics integration** — doesn't connect training to actual results

- **Day 90 retention for SaaS:** 10-25%

### What Users Love

### Limitations for Coaching/Accountability
- "Sustainable accountability doesn't come from better tracking tools — it comes from culture"
- PM tools track tasks, not outcomes or personal growth
- No coaching layer — no one telling you what your priorities SHOULD be
- No assessment capability — can't diagnose what needs attention
- No content/training integration — purely operational
- "Every team and project is unique — there is no simple solution"

### GAP: What's Missing
- **No prescriptive daily protocol** — they're blank canvases, not guided systems
- **No coaching intelligence** — tool tells you if a task is late, not WHY you're stuck
- **No personal development integration** — purely task-based
- **No business assessment** — can't diagnose organizational health
- **No content or training** — operational only
- **No financial intelligence** — basic dashboards, not P&L integration
- **Require significant setup/customization** — most SMBs never configure them properly

## 7. Emerging AI-Powered Platforms

### Major AI Coaching Platforms (2025-2026)

#### CoachVox AI
- **What it does:** Lets coaches train an AI clone on their knowledge and voice
- **Daily protocol:** White-label AI chatbot answers questions, runs exercises, nurtures leads 24/7
- **Rating:** 4.5/5 from verified users
- **Pricing:** Premium (not widely disclosed)
- **Strength:** "AI sounds like them rather than a generic assistant"
- **Weakness:** For coaches to scale their practice — not a standalone daily system for users
- **Saves coaches 5-10 hours/week** through FAQ handling and chat

#### Rocky.ai
- **What it does:** AI-powered personal development coaching (positive psychology based)
- **Daily protocol:** Daily micro-coaching sessions with psychologist-designed questions
- **Rating:** 4.24/5 from 17 verified users on AppSumo
- **Pricing:** From $10/user/month (white-label)
- **Strength:** "Intuitive AI providing personalized insights" for self-awareness and goal clarity
- **Weakness:** "Cluttered UI," slight learning curve, personal development focus (not business operations)

#### BetterUp
- **What it does:** "Human + AI Coaching for All" — enterprise-focused leadership coaching
- **Daily protocol:** Proactive AI coaching in the flow of work
- **Scale:** 4+ million coaching sessions of data powering the AI
- **Pricing:** Enterprise pricing (not disclosed publicly)
- **Strength:** Combines human coaches with AI at scale
- **Weakness:** Enterprise-focused, not SMB/entrepreneur accessible

#### Valence (Nadia AI)
- **What it does:** AI coach customizable for talent priorities, understands company values/culture
- **Daily protocol:** Proactive help with goals, feedback, skills, conflict, meeting prep, role-playing
- **Strength:** "Only AI coach fully customizable for any talent priority"
- **Weakness:** Corporate/enterprise focused, not entrepreneur-oriented

#### CoachHub (AIMY)
- **What it does:** AI coach for interactive coaching conversations
- **Strength:** Built on proven coaching methodologies + cutting-edge tech
- **Weakness:** Enterprise employee development, not business owner accountability

#### Nag Bot
- **What it does:** AI accountability partner that adapts to your life, remembers progress
- **Pricing:** Free (8 messages/day), Premium $12.99/month or $129.99/year
- **Strength:** Simple, persistent, remembers context
- **Weakness:** Consumer app, no business framework

#### Overlord
- **What it does:** "Hardcore" AI accountability — monitors habits 24/7, screen blocking, real consequences
- **Strength:** Aggressive accountability approach
- **Weakness:** Punitive rather than coaching-oriented

#### NeverGiveUp
- **What it does:** 24/7 WhatsApp coaching — AI + human monitoring
- **Pricing:** From $9/week
- **Strength:** Meets people where they are (WhatsApp)
- **Weakness:** Message-based only, no integrated system

### Industry Statistics
- **AI in HR market:** Growing from $8.16B (2025) to $30.77B by 2034 (15.94% CAGR)
- **Coaching completion boost:** Commitment check-ins push follow-through from 65% to 95%
- **Key insight:** "AI coaching went from niche concept to crowded market category almost overnight"
- **The gap between human coaching demand and supply has created conditions for AI coaching to grow rapidly**

### GAP: What's Missing Across ALL AI Platforms
- **NONE combine: assessment + daily protocol + business operations + personal development + AI coaching**
- Enterprise platforms (BetterUp, Valence, CoachHub) are inaccessible to SMBs
- Consumer apps (Rocky, Nag Bot) are personal, not business-operational
- Coach-scaling tools (CoachVox) are B2B2C, not direct-to-user
- **No platform integrates financial data with coaching protocols**
- **No platform provides a prescriptive daily CEO operating system powered by AI**
- **No platform connects assessment diagnostics to automated daily task generation**

## 9. Four Pillars App Opportunity Map

### The Unfilled Market Position

**NO PLATFORM currently delivers:**
1. A diagnostic assessment that identifies your specific business/personal gaps
2. A daily 15-30 minute prescriptive protocol generated from that assessment
3. Integration of personal development AND business operations in one system
4. AI-powered coaching that adapts based on your actual progress data
5. Financial intelligence connected to coaching protocols
6. Team accountability features accessible to businesses under 50 employees
7. All of the above at $30-$99/month (accessible to SMBs)

### Competitive Advantages to Build

| Advantage | How It's Unique |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Assessment-First** | Unlike content-first platforms (Cardone, Elliott), you diagnose THEN prescribe |
| **Daily CEO Protocol** | Unlike EOS (team meetings), you deliver individual daily operating rhythms |
| **Four-Pillar Integration** | Unlike GrowthDay (personal only) or EOS (business only), you integrate both |
| **AI-Adaptive** | Unlike static content libraries, the protocol evolves as you progress |
| **SMB-Priced** | Unlike $50K EOS implementations or $35K workshops, accessible at subscription price |
| **Financial Intelligence** | Unlike any coaching platform, connected to actual business financial data |
| **Progress Scoring** | Like GrowthDay's life scores but for BUSINESS performance across four pillars |

### Pricing Sweet Spot
- **$49-$79/month** — above GrowthDay ($25-50), below Elliott ($299)
- Positioned as "less than a business coach lunch" or "$2-$3/day"
- Annual discount to $399-$599/year for retention
- Premium tier with AI coaching + human accountability partner at $149-$199/month

### Critical Success Factors (From Competitor Analysis)
1. **Daily time investment must be <20 minutes** — GrowthDay proves "10-15 min" works
2. **Visible progress scoring** — GrowthDay's life scores create return habits
3. **Community element** — Skool/Hormozi prove community reduces churn by 10%+
4. **Commitment check-ins boost completion from 65% to 95%** — build this in
5. **Don't require team adoption first** — EOS's team dependency causes 66% failure
6. **Avoid content-library trap** — Cardone/Elliott prove videos alone don't retain
7. **Assessment must feel valuable independently** — even before the daily protocol, the diagnosis should deliver insight

### Key Takeaway
> **The market has coaching programs that teach, tools that track, and AI that chats. Nobody has built the integrated daily operating system that TELLS a business owner exactly what to do today based on where they actually are across all four pillars of their business and life.**
