## 1.1 The Daily Marketing Check-In (15â€“20 Minutes)

**Framework: The "Morning Marketing Pulse"**
*Time: First 15 minutes of the workday*

### What to Check Daily â€” The 7-Point Morning Protocol

| # | Check | Where to Look | Action Threshold | Time |
|---|-------|---------------|-----------------|------|
| 1 | **Ad Spend vs. Budget** | Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads | If >110% of daily budget pace, pause lowest performer | 2 min |
| 2 | **Cost Per Lead (CPL)** | CRM dashboard / ad platform | If CPL >2x target, investigate immediately | 2 min |
| 3 | **Lead Volume (last 24h)** | CRM inbox / form submissions | If <50% of daily target, check ad delivery | 2 min |
| 4 | **New Leads Responded To** | CRM / inbox | Any lead >5 min old without response = escalate | 3 min |
| 5 | **Appointment Show Rate** | Calendar / CRM | If <60% yesterday, trigger reminder sequence review | 2 min |
| 6 | **Revenue Closed (yesterday)** | CRM pipeline / payment processor | Log against monthly target | 1 min |
| 7 | **Content Published?** | Social scheduler / blog CMS | Confirm today's content is queued and live | 1 min |

### Daily Decision Matrix

```
IF CPL is rising + CTR is dropping â†’ Creative fatigue. Refresh ad creative.
IF CPL is rising + CTR is stable â†’ Audience saturation. Expand or rotate audiences.
IF CPL is stable + lead quality dropping â†’ Funnel/offer mismatch. Review landing page.
IF leads are up + appointments down â†’ Nurture sequence broken. Check email/SMS automation.
IF appointments up + close rate down â†’ Sales process issue, not marketing.
```

### What the CEO Needs to See Daily
- Total leads generated (vs. target)
- Total ad spend (vs. budget)
- Revenue closed
- Pipeline value

### What the Marketing Person Needs to See Daily
- All 7 points above plus: ad frequency scores, email deliverability, landing page load time, form completion rates, individual ad performance

## 2.3 UTM Parameter Strategy for Multi-Channel Attribution

### The UTM Framework

UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters are tags added to URLs that tell analytics platforms where traffic came from.

### Standard UTM Parameters

| Parameter | Purpose | Required? | Example Values |
|-----------|---------|-----------|----------------|
| `utm_source` | Which platform/site | Yes | `facebook`, `google`, `email`, `linkedin` |
| `utm_medium` | Marketing medium type | Yes | `cpc`, `organic`, `email`, `social`, `referral` |
| `utm_campaign` | Campaign name | Yes | `spring-sale-2026`, `webinar-launch-feb` |
| `utm_content` | Differentiates ad creative | Recommended | `video-testimonial-v1`, `carousel-benefits` |
| `utm_term` | Keyword (for search ads) | Optional | `business-coaching`, `marketing-consultant` |

### Naming Convention Best Practices

**Rules**:
1. Always lowercase (Google Analytics is case-sensitive)
2. Use hyphens, not spaces or underscores
3. Be consistent â€” document your conventions
4. Include date or version when testing

**Recommended Naming Structure**:
```
utm_source=platform
utm_medium=channel-type
utm_campaign=yyyy-mm-campaign-name
utm_content=creative-type-variant
utm_term=keyword-or-audience
```

**Examples**:

| Scenario | Full UTM String |
|----------|----------------|
| Facebook lead ad, Feb 2026, video creative v2 | `?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=2026-02-lead-gen&utm_content=video-testimonial-v2&utm_term=lookalike-buyers` |
| Google Search ad for coaching | `?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=2026-02-coaching-search&utm_content=headline-roi-focused&utm_term=business-coaching-near-me` |
| Email newsletter, weekly digest | `?utm_source=email&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=2026-02-weekly-digest-w1&utm_content=cta-button-top` |
| Instagram organic post | `?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=organic-social&utm_campaign=2026-02-tips-series&utm_content=carousel-5-tips` |

### Building a UTM Tracking System

1. **Create a UTM Master Sheet** (Google Sheet shared with team)
   - Columns: Date, Source, Medium, Campaign, Content, Term, Full URL, Notes
   - Use Google's Campaign URL Builder or a custom sheet formula
2. **Use a URL shortener** for clean links (Bitly, Short.io)
3. **Connect to GA4** â€” UTMs automatically populate in GA4 under Acquisition > Traffic Acquisition
4. **Monthly audit**: Check GA4 for "(not set)" or inconsistent UTM values

## 2.4 Attribution Models â€” Which to Use When

### The Three Core Models

#### First-Touch Attribution
**What it measures**: The first interaction that introduced the lead to your business
**Best for**: Understanding which channels generate awareness and new leads
**Use when**: You want to know "Where did this person first find us?"

```
Example: Jane sees a Facebook ad â†’ clicks â†’ later Googles your name â†’ books a call
First-Touch Credit: 100% to Facebook
```

**Pros**: Simple; shows top-of-funnel effectiveness
**Cons**: Ignores everything that happened after first touch

#### Last-Touch Attribution
**What it measures**: The final interaction before conversion
**Best for**: Understanding which channels close deals
**Use when**: You want to know "What was the last thing they did before buying?"

```
Example: Jane sees a Facebook ad â†’ reads 3 emails â†’ clicks Google ad â†’ books a call
Last-Touch Credit: 100% to Google Ads
```

**Pros**: Simple; shows bottom-of-funnel effectiveness
**Cons**: Ignores the nurture journey

#### Multi-Touch Attribution
**What it measures**: Credit distributed across all touchpoints
**Best for**: Understanding the full customer journey
**Use when**: You have multiple channels and want to optimize the entire funnel

**Common multi-touch models**:
- **Linear**: Equal credit to all touchpoints
- **Time-Decay**: More credit to touchpoints closer to conversion
- **U-Shaped (Position-Based)**: 40% to first touch, 40% to last touch, 20% split among middle
- **W-Shaped**: 30% first, 30% lead creation, 30% opportunity creation, 10% middle
- **Data-Driven (GA4)**: Machine learning distributes credit based on actual conversion patterns

## 4.6 The "Soap Opera Sequence" (Russell Brunson)

**Source**: "DotCom Secrets" by Russell Brunson
**Purpose**: Transform a cold email subscriber into an emotionally invested follower ready to buy

### The Framework

The Soap Opera Sequence (SOS) uses serialized storytelling â€” like a soap opera â€” where each email ends with a cliffhanger that compels the reader to open the next one.

### The 5-Email Soap Opera Sequence

| Email # | Name | Purpose | Key Elements |
|---------|------|---------|-------------|
| **1** | **Set the Stage** | Build curiosity and rapport | Introduce yourself; hint at a transformative backstory; set expectations ("Over the next few days, I'm going to share something that changed everything for me..."); end with cliffhanger |
| **2** | **High Drama / Backstory** | Create emotional connection | Share your origin story â€” the struggle, the rock bottom moment, the breakthrough; make it personal and vulnerable; end with "But here's what I discovered..." |
| **3** | **Epiphany** | Reveal the key insight | Share the "aha moment" that changed everything; this is the core framework/method/belief; position your offer as the vehicle for this transformation; end with "But I almost didn't make it because..." |
| **4** | **Hidden Benefits** | Stack value and overcome objections | Share unexpected benefits of the solution; address objections indirectly through story ("I was worried about X too, but then..."); social proof woven into narrative |
| **5** | **Urgency + CTA** | Drive action | Create urgency (deadline, scarcity, or emotional urgency); clear call to action; remind them of the transformation; "Here's what to do next..." |

### SOS Implementation Tips
- **Send daily** (Days 1â€“5) â€” momentum matters more than spacing
- **Subject lines should create curiosity**, not reveal the content
- **Each email should be 300â€“500 words** â€” long enough to tell a story, short enough to maintain attention
- **Always end with a cliffhanger** or open loop (except Email 5)
- **Voice should be conversational** â€” write like you're emailing a friend
- After the SOS, transition into the "Seinfeld Sequence" (daily value emails about "nothing" â€” lifestyle, tips, stories)

# Appendix B: Formula Reference

### Core Marketing Formulas

```
CPL (Cost Per Lead) = Total Ad Spend / Total Leads Generated

CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) = Total Marketing Spend / Total New Customers

ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend

ROI = (Revenue - Total Cost) / Total Cost Ã— 100

LTV (Lifetime Value) = Average Purchase Value Ã— Purchase Frequency Ã— Customer Lifespan

LTV:CAC Ratio = Customer Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost
    â†’ Target: 3:1 or higher

Break-Even ROAS = 1 / Profit Margin
    â†’ e.g., 50% margin = 2:1 break-even ROAS

Marketing Efficiency Ratio (MER) = Total Revenue / Total Marketing Spend
    â†’ Unlike ROAS (ad-specific), MER includes ALL marketing costs

Pipeline Velocity = (# of Opportunities Ã— Average Deal Value Ã— Win Rate) / Sales Cycle Length

Expected Monthly Customers = Traffic Ã— Conversion Rate Ã— Close Rate Ã— Show Rate
```

### The "Reverse Engineering" Formula (Setting Targets)

```
GOAL: $50,000/month revenue
Average deal value: $2,500
Customers needed: $50,000 / $2,500 = 20 customers

Close rate: 30%
Appointments needed: 20 / 0.30 = 67 appointments

Show rate: 75%
Bookings needed: 67 / 0.75 = 89 bookings

Lead-to-booking rate: 25%
Leads needed: 89 / 0.25 = 356 leads

Landing page conversion: 30%
Website visitors needed: 356 / 0.30 = 1,187 visitors

Cost per click: $2.00
Ad budget needed: 1,187 Ã— $2.00 = $2,374/month

Revenue / Spend = $50,000 / $2,374 = 21:1 ROAS

This math makes every target specific and measurable.
```
