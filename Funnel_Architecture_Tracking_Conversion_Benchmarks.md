## 1. The DemandGen Waterfall Model

### Origin & Creator

The **Demand Waterfall** was created by **SiriusDecisions** (now part of Forrester Research). It was originally launched in **2002** and significantly revised in **2012** into the "Rearchitected Demand Waterfall," which became the de facto standard for managing B2B demand generation processes.

**Key architect:** Terry Flaherty, VP and Principal Analyst at SiriusDecisions, led the rearchitected version.

### How It Works

The Demand Waterfall is a structured framework that tracks and manages the progression of leads from initial inquiry through to closed revenue. It provides forward-looking metrics and leading indicators that go beyond engagement volumes to provide insights into lead quality at each stage.

SiriusDecisions recommends monitoring conversions via three complementary pathways:
- **MGL (Marketing Generated Leads)** -- leads sourced by marketing
- **TGL (Teleprospecting Generated Leads)** -- leads sourced by outbound teleprospecting
- **SGL (Sales Generated Leads)** -- leads sourced directly by sales

### All Stages of the Rearchitected Waterfall (2012)

| Stage | Abbreviation | Definition |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Inquiry** | INQ | Initial engagement with any marketing program (form fill, content download, event registration) |
| **Automation Qualified Lead** | AQL | Marketing automation scores the inquiry based on fit + behavior to determine readiness for human follow-up |
| **Teleprospecting Accepted Lead** | TAL | A dedicated teleprospecting team evaluates the AQL and accepts or rejects it based on data quality and verification |
| **Teleprospecting Qualified Lead** | TQL | The teleprospecting team has made live contact and confirmed qualification criteria (budget, authority, need, timeline) |
| **Marketing Qualified Lead** | MQL | Lead meets both behavioral scoring thresholds AND teleprospecting qualification -- ready for sales |
| **Sales Accepted Lead** | SAL | Sales rep accepts the lead into their pipeline for active pursuit |
| **Sales Qualified Lead** | SQL | Sales has created a formal opportunity with a dollar value and estimated close date |
| **Sales Qualified Opportunity** | SQO | The opportunity has moved beyond pre-qualification into a fully qualified pipeline stage with validated requirements |
| **Closed/Won** | CW | Revenue is booked |

### Conversion Rate Benchmarks Per Stage

| Stage Transition | Benchmark Rate | Context |
|-----------------|---------------|---------|
| **INQ to MQL** | **10%** | Aggregate benchmark across the demand waterfall |
| **INQ to TQL (new concept/emerging market)** | **15-25%** | Higher because prospects are curious about novel solutions |
| **INQ to TQL (mature market)** | **5-7%** | Lower because prospects are already educated and more selective |
| **MQL to SQO** | **30%** | 30% of MQLs are expected to become sales qualified opportunities |
| **MQL to SQL** | **13%** | Median across industries (First Page Sage 2026 report) |
| **MQL to SQL (by source -- website leads)** | **31.3%** | Highest converting source |
| **MQL to SQL (by source -- referrals)** | **24.7%** | Second highest converting source |
| **MQL to SQL (by source -- webinars)** | **17.8%** | Strong mid-funnel source |
| **MQL to SQL (by source -- events)** | **4.2%** | Low despite high engagement |
| **MQL to SQL (by source -- email campaigns)** | **0.9%** | Lowest converting source |
| **MQL to SQL (by source -- lead lists)** | **2.5%** | Low quality source |
| **TQL to Closed/Won (new concept)** | **2.5-4.5%** | Low due to funding and priority challenges |
| **TQL to Closed/Won (mature market)** | **12-18%** | Higher due to funded initiatives and established demand |

**Critical Insight from Forrester:** There is no universal "best-in-class" benchmark. Waterfall benchmarks must be aligned to your specific context -- your market maturity, deal complexity, and sales cycle length.

## 2. Russell Brunson Funnel Types & Conversion Benchmarks

### Creator & Context

**Russell Brunson** is the co-founder of ClickFunnels and author of *DotCom Secrets*, *Expert Secrets*, and *Traffic Secrets*. He developed the **Value Ladder** framework, which he used to grow ClickFunnels from $0 to over $100M in annual revenue. The Value Ladder has four tiers:

1. **Bait** -- Free lead magnet offered in exchange for an email address
2. **Frontend** -- Least expensive, least valuable product/service ($7-$47)
3. **Middle** -- More expensive, more valuable product/service ($97-$997)
4. **Backend** -- Most expensive, most valuable product/service ($997-$25,000+)

## 3. UTM Parameter Strategy for Multi-Channel Attribution

### What Are UTM Parameters?

UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters are tags appended to URLs that allow analytics tools (especially Google Analytics 4) to identify the exact source, medium, campaign, content variant, and keyword that drove a visitor to your site.

### The Five Standard UTM Parameters

| Parameter | Purpose | Required? | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| `utm_source` | Identifies the traffic origin (platform/publisher) | Yes | `facebook`, `google`, `linkedin`, `newsletter` |
| `utm_medium` | Identifies the marketing channel type | Yes | `cpc`, `email`, `social`, `organic`, `referral` |
| `utm_campaign` | Identifies the specific campaign or promotion | Yes | `spring-sale-2025`, `webinar-launch-q1` |
| `utm_content` | Differentiates creative variants or links | No | `carousel-ad-a`, `hero-banner`, `sidebar-cta` |
| `utm_term` | Identifies paid search keywords or targeting terms | No | `running-shoes`, `b2b-saas-leads` |

### Exact Naming Convention Best Practices

**Rule 1: Always Use Lowercase**
```
CORRECT:  utm_source=facebook
WRONG:    utm_source=Facebook
WRONG:    utm_source=FACEBOOK
```
GA4 treats `Facebook`, `facebook`, and `FACEBOOK` as three different sources. This splinters your data.

**Rule 2: Use Hyphens as Separators (Not Underscores or Spaces)**
```
CORRECT:  utm_campaign=spring-sale-2025
WRONG:    utm_campaign=spring_sale_2025
WRONG:    utm_campaign=spring%20sale%202025
```

**Rule 3: Standardize Source Names Across Teams**
```
CORRECT:  utm_source=facebook (used by ALL team members)
WRONG:    utm_source=fb (used by some) AND utm_source=facebook (used by others)
```

**Rule 4: Limit Parameters to 100 Characters** to prevent iOS truncation.

### Recommended Naming Convention Structure

**Model 1: Hierarchical Naming (Recommended for Small Businesses)**

```
utm_source=[platform]
utm_medium=[channel-type]
utm_campaign=[initiative]-[date]-[audience]
utm_content=[creative-variant]-[placement]
utm_term=[targeting-keyword]
```

**Full URL Example:**
```
https://yoursite.com/landing-page?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=webinar-launch-jan2025-cold&utm_content=carousel-ad-v2-feed&utm_term=business-coaching
```

**Model 2: Key-Value Naming (Recommended for Multi-Channel Teams)**

Embeds descriptive pairs directly:
```
utm_campaign=la-en!t-shopping!c-retargeting
```
Where `la` = location, `en` = language, `t` = type, `c` = category. Highly flexible and scalable.

### Standard utm_source Values (Use Consistently)

| Platform | utm_source Value |
|----------|-----------------|
| Facebook/Instagram Ads | `facebook` |
| Google Ads | `google` |
| LinkedIn Ads | `linkedin` |
| TikTok Ads | `tiktok` |

| Email Automation | `email-automation` |
| Podcast | `podcast` |

### Standard utm_medium Values

| Channel Type | utm_medium Value |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Paid Search | `cpc` |
| Paid Social | `paid-social` |
| Organic Social | `social` |

### Documentation & Governance

- **Store naming guidelines** in a shared repository (Notion, Google Sheets, or Confluence)
- **Create a UTM builder spreadsheet** where team members input parameters to generate tagged URLs

- Companies with standardized naming conventions see a **29% improvement** in campaign attribution accuracy

## 4. Attribution Models: First-Touch vs. Last-Touch vs. Multi-Touch

### Model Comparison Matrix

| Feature | First-Touch | Last-Touch | Multi-Touch |
|---------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| **Credit Assignment** | 100% to first interaction | 100% to last interaction | Distributed across multiple touchpoints |
| **Complexity** | Very Low | Very Low | Medium to High |
| **Best For** | Top-of-funnel evaluation | Bottom-of-funnel evaluation | Full-journey understanding |
| **Data Required** | Minimal | Minimal | Significant |
| **Cost to Implement** | Free (GA4 built-in) | Free (GA4 built-in) | Free to $$$ depending on model |
| **Accuracy** | Low (ignores 90%+ of journey) | Low (ignores 90%+ of journey) | High (considers full journey) |

### First-Touch Attribution

**How it works:** Gives 100% of conversion credit to the very first interaction a customer had with your brand.

**When to use:**
- Evaluating **which channels are best at attracting NEW customers**
- Measuring **brand awareness campaigns**
- Assessing **top-of-funnel content performance**
- When you need to **justify awareness spending** to stakeholders

**Pros:**
- Simple to implement and understand
- Clearly identifies discovery channels
- Useful for brand-building measurement
- Available natively in GA4

**Cons:**
- Completely ignores the nurturing journey
- Over-credits awareness channels, under-credits conversion channels
- Misleading for long sales cycles
- Does not reflect how B2B buying actually works

### Last-Touch Attribution

**How it works:** Gives 100% of conversion credit to the final interaction before conversion.

**When to use:**
- Short sales cycles (e-commerce, impulse purchases)
- **Direct response campaigns** where you need to know "what closed the deal"
- As a **starting point** when resources are limited
- Measuring **bottom-of-funnel campaign effectiveness**

**Pros:**
- Simplest to track and most actionable
- Often the most accurate for short-cycle businesses
- Default model in many platforms (Google Ads, Facebook Ads)
- Best for businesses with limited marketing touchpoints

**Cons:**
- Ignores all supporting touchpoints
- Under-credits brand awareness and nurturing efforts
- Can lead to over-investment in bottom-funnel tactics
- Misleading for high-ticket or long-cycle businesses

### Multi-Touch Attribution (MTA) Models

**How it works:** Distributes conversion credit across multiple touchpoints in the customer journey.

#### Sub-Models:

**1. Linear Attribution**
- Gives **equal credit** to every touchpoint
- Example: 5 touchpoints = 20% credit each
- **Best for:** Getting a baseline view of all channels; when you have no hypothesis about which touchpoints matter most
- **Weakness:** Does not differentiate high-impact vs. low-impact touches

**2. Time Decay Attribution**
- Gives **more credit to touchpoints closer to conversion**
- Example: Email offer = 50%, LinkedIn ad = 30%, whitepaper = 15%, webinar = 5%
- **Best for:** Businesses with long sales cycles where recent interactions are most influential; B2B SaaS
- **Weakness:** Under-credits awareness-stage activities

**3. Position-Based (U-Shaped) Attribution**
- Gives **40% to first touch, 40% to last touch, 20% split among middle touches**
- **Best for:** When you believe introduction and conversion moments are most important
- **Weakness:** Arbitrary weighting; under-credits nurturing

**4. W-Shaped Attribution**
- Gives **30% to first touch, 30% to lead creation, 30% to opportunity creation, 10% split among other touches**
- **Best for:** B2B companies with defined lead generation stages
- **Weakness:** Requires accurate stage tracking in CRM

**5. Data-Driven / Algorithmic Attribution**
- Uses **machine learning** to determine credit based on actual data patterns
- Available in GA4 (requires sufficient data volume)
- **Best for:** Companies with high traffic volume and multiple channels
- **Weakness:** Requires large datasets; can be a "black box"

### Which Model to Use -- Decision Framework for Small Businesses

| Business Scenario | Recommended Model | Reasoning |
|------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Just starting out, limited data | **Last-Touch** | Simple, actionable, builds baseline understanding |
| Short sales cycle (under 7 days) | **Last-Touch** | Few touchpoints to track; last touch is usually decisive |
| Running brand awareness + conversion campaigns | **Position-Based (U-Shaped)** | Credits both discovery and conversion moments |
| Long B2B sales cycle (30+ days) | **W-Shaped** or **Time Decay** | Recognizes key stage transitions |
| High traffic volume, multiple channels | **Data-Driven** (GA4) | Let algorithms determine actual impact |
| Evaluating top-of-funnel spend | **First-Touch** (alongside another model) | Use as supplementary view, not primary |

## 5. Conversion Tracking Setup

### 5A. Google Analytics 4 (GA4) Setup

**Important Context:** According to a 2025 Semrush study, **73% of marketing teams struggle with GA4 setup**. GA4 uses an event-based model (not session-based like the old Universal Analytics), tracking each user interaction as a single event.

#### Step 1: Create Your GA4 Property

1. Go to [analytics.google.com](https://analytics.google.com)
2. Click **Admin** (gear icon, bottom left)
3. Click **Create Property**
4. Enter property name, select time zone and currency
5. Complete business details and objectives
6. Choose your platform (Web, iOS, Android)
7. Enter your website URL
8. Copy the **Measurement ID** (format: G-XXXXXXXXXX)

#### Step 2: Install the GA4 Tag on Your Website

**Option A: Direct Installation (gtag.js)**
```html
<!-- Google tag (gtag.js) -->
<script async src="https://www.googletagmanager.com/gtag/js?id=G-XXXXXXXXXX"></script>
<script>
  window.dataLayer = window.dataLayer || [];
  function gtag(){dataLayer.push(arguments);}
  gtag('js', new Date());
  gtag('config', 'G-XXXXXXXXXX');
</script>
```
Place this code in the `<head>` section of every page.

**Option B: Google Tag Manager (Recommended)**
1. Create a GTM account at [tagmanager.google.com](https://tagmanager.google.com)
2. Install the GTM container code on your site
3. In GTM, create a new tag: **Google Analytics: GA4 Configuration**
4. Enter your Measurement ID
5. Set trigger to **All Pages**
6. Publish the container

#### Step 3: Set Up Conversion Events (Key Events)

**Navigate to:** Admin > Events > Create Event

**GA4 Recommended Events for Small Businesses:**

| Event Name | Use Case | Parameters |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| `generate_lead` | Form submissions, lead captures | `value`, `currency` |
| `sign_up` | Account registrations | `method` |
| `purchase` | Completed transactions | `transaction_id`, `value`, `currency`, `items` |
| `begin_checkout` | Started checkout process | `value`, `currency`, `items` |
| `add_to_cart` | Added item to cart | `items`, `value` |
| `view_item` | Viewed a product/service page | `items` |
| `page_view` | (Automatic) Page loads | `page_location`, `page_title` |
| `scroll` | (Automatic) 90% scroll depth | -- |
| `click` | (Automatic) Outbound link clicks | `link_url` |
| `file_download` | (Automatic) File downloads | `file_name`, `file_extension` |

**Custom Event Example (Contact Form Submission):**
1. Go to **Admin > Events > Create Event**
2. Event name: `form_submit_contact`
3. Condition: `event_name` equals `page_view` AND `page_location` contains `thank-you`
4. Click **Create**

#### Step 4: Mark Events as Conversions (Key Events)

1. Go to **Admin > Events**
2. Find your event in the list
3. Toggle **"Mark as key event"** to ON
4. Events marked as key events will appear in conversion reports

#### Step 5: Add Monetary Values to Conversions

1. Go to **Admin > Events**
2. Click your conversion event
3. Add parameter: `value` with dollar amount (e.g., average deal value)
4. Add parameter: `currency` = `USD`

#### Step 6: Verify Everything Works

1. **DebugView:** Admin > DebugView -- test events in real-time
2. **Realtime Report:** Reports > Realtime -- see live user events
3. **Standard Reports:** Reports > Engagement > Conversions (populate within 24-48 hours)

#### Step 7: Connect GA4 to Google Ads

1. In Google Ads: **Tools > Conversions > Import > GA4 Events**
2. Select the conversions you marked in GA4
3. **Critical:** Only import conversions once -- either from GA4 OR directly from Ads, NOT both (avoids double counting)

### 5B. Facebook (Meta) Pixel & Conversions API Implementation

**Why Both Are Required:** If you rely only on the Meta Pixel, you are almost certainly missing conversions due to iOS privacy restrictions, consent banners, and ad blockers. Businesses see **20-40% more conversions tracked** after implementing server-side tracking.

#### Part 1: Meta Pixel Setup (Client-Side)

1. Go to **Meta Events Manager** (business.facebook.com/events_manager)
2. Click **Connect Data Sources > Web > Connect**
3. Name your Pixel and add your website URL
4. Choose **"Meta Pixel and Conversions API"** (recommended dual setup)
5. Copy the Pixel base code
6. Paste the code into the `<head>` section of your website, above the `</head>` tag

**Standard Events to Configure:**

| Event Name | Trigger | Code |
|-----------|---------|------|
| `PageView` | Every page load | Automatic with base code |
| `Lead` | Form submission | `fbq('track', 'Lead');` |
| `CompleteRegistration` | Signup/registration | `fbq('track', 'CompleteRegistration');` |
| `Schedule` | Booking/appointment | `fbq('track', 'Schedule');` |
| `Purchase` | Transaction complete | `fbq('track', 'Purchase', {value: 99.00, currency: 'USD'});` |
| `ViewContent` | Key page views | `fbq('track', 'ViewContent');` |
| `AddToCart` | Cart additions | `fbq('track', 'AddToCart');` |
| `InitiateCheckout` | Checkout start | `fbq('track', 'InitiateCheckout');` |

#### Part 2: Meta Conversions API (Server-Side)

**Method 1: Via Server-Side Google Tag Manager (Recommended)**

1. Create a **Server-Side GTM Container** at [tagmanager.google.com](https://tagmanager.google.com)
2. Deploy the server container to **Google Cloud Platform** or **Stape.io** (easier, ~$20/month)
3. Connect your server container to a **custom subdomain** (e.g., `analytics.yoursite.com`)
4. In Server-Side GTM, create a **Facebook Conversions API tag**
5. Enter your **Pixel ID** and **Access Token** (from Events Manager > Settings)
6. Map events from your client-side GA4 tag to Conversions API events

**Method 2: Native Platform Integrations**

Shopify, WordPress (via plugins like PixelYourSite), and major e-commerce platforms have built-in Conversions API support.

#### Part 3: Event Deduplication (Critical)

When using both Pixel and Conversions API, the same event can be counted twice. **Solution:**

1. Assign each conversion a **unique event_id** (e.g., order ID, transaction ID, or generated UUID)
2. Include this `event_id` in BOTH your Pixel event AND your Conversions API event
3. Facebook uses this ID to recognize duplicates and counts each event only once

```javascript
// Client-side Pixel with event_id
fbq('track', 'Purchase', {
  value: 99.00,
  currency: 'USD'
}, {eventID: 'order_12345'});

// Server-side CAPI sends the same event_id: 'order_12345'
```

#### Part 4: Improve Event Match Quality

Send **hashed first-party identifiers** with every event:
- Hashed email address
- Hashed phone number
- Client IP address
- User agent string
- Facebook click ID (fbc)
- Facebook browser ID (fbp)

Higher Event Match Quality = better attribution = lower CPAs.

### 5C. Server-Side Tracking Setup

**Why Server-Side Tracking Matters:** Client-side tracking (browser-based) is increasingly blocked by ad blockers, iOS privacy features, and cookie consent requirements. Server-side tracking sends data directly from your server to analytics platforms, **bypassing all browser-based limitations**.

#### Architecture Overview

```
User's Browser --> Your Web Server --> Server-Side GTM Container --> GA4, Meta, Google Ads, etc.
                                           |
                                    (runs on your subdomain:
                                     analytics.yoursite.com)
```

#### Setup Steps (Server-Side GTM)

1. **Create Server Container:** In GTM, create a new container with type "Server"
2. **Provision Server:** Deploy to Google Cloud Run (auto-scaling) or Stape.io (managed service)
3. **Custom Domain:** Point a subdomain like `data.yoursite.com` to your server container URL
4. **Configure Client-Side to Send Data to Server:**
   ```javascript
   gtag('config', 'G-XXXXXXXXXX', {
     server_container_url: 'https://data.yoursite.com'
   });
   ```
5. **Set Up Server-Side Tags:**
   - GA4 server-side tag
   - Meta Conversions API tag
   - Google Ads conversion tag
   - Any other destination tags
6. **Data Enrichment:** Add server-side variables (user ID, CRM data, transaction values)
7. **PII Scrubbing:** Remove personally identifiable information before forwarding to third parties

#### Benefits of Server-Side Tracking

- **20-40% more conversions tracked** compared to client-side only
- **First-party cookies** last longer (up to 2 years vs. 7 days with ITP)
- **Full data control** -- scrub PII before sending to vendors
- **Reduced page load time** -- fewer scripts running in the browser
- **Ad blocker bypass** -- tracking requests come from your own domain

## 6. The Weekly Funnel Audit Protocol

### Overview

The best-performing companies audit their funnels **quarterly at minimum** (per Altior & Co.), but a **weekly micro-audit** catches problems before they compound into revenue losses. The protocol below synthesizes best practices from multiple frameworks.

### Weekly Funnel Audit -- Complete Protocol

---

#### Phase 1: Data Integrity Check (Monday -- 15 minutes)

| # | Check Item | What to Look For | Fix |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| 1 | **Tracking pixels firing** | Verify GA4, Meta Pixel, and all platform tags are active on all pages | Re-install tags; check GTM container is published |
| 2 | **UTM parameters intact** | Confirm active campaign URLs have correct, consistent UTMs | Fix broken URLs; update UTM spreadsheet |
| 3 | **Conversion events recording** | Check GA4 Realtime report and Meta Events Manager for event activity | Recreate broken events; test with DebugView |
| 4 | **Form submissions flowing to CRM** | Submit a test lead and verify it arrives in your CRM within 5 minutes | Check Zapier/integration connections; rebuild automations |
| 5 | **Email automation triggers** | Verify new leads enter the correct email sequence | Re-map trigger conditions in your ESP |

---

#### Phase 2: Top-of-Funnel Review (Tuesday -- 20 minutes)

| # | Check Item | What to Look For | Fix |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| 6 | **Ad performance metrics** | CTR, CPC, CPM, frequency, relevance score across all active campaigns | Pause underperforming ads (<1% CTR); refresh creative on high-frequency ads |
| 7 | **Landing page conversion rate** | Compare to benchmarks (5-15% standard; 18%+ for lead magnets) | A/B test headlines, CTAs, and lead magnet offers |
| 8 | **Traffic volume by source** | Are all channels delivering expected traffic? Any sudden drops? | Investigate algorithm changes; increase budget on winning channels |
| 9 | **Cost per lead by channel** | Identify which channels produce leads most efficiently | Reallocate budget from high-CPL to low-CPL channels |
| 10 | **Lead magnet performance** | Download/opt-in rates by lead magnet type | Retire low performers; double down on high performers |

---

#### Phase 3: Middle-of-Funnel Review (Wednesday -- 20 minutes)

| # | Check Item | What to Look For | Fix |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| 11 | **Email open rates** | Benchmark: 20-30%; identify sequences below threshold | Rewrite subject lines; adjust send times; clean list |
| 12 | **Email click-through rates** | Benchmark: 2-5%; identify low-performing emails | Improve CTAs; test different content formats; shorten emails |
| 13 | **Lead-to-MQL conversion rate** | Are leads progressing or stalling? | Adjust lead scoring criteria; add nurture touchpoints |
| 14 | **Speed to lead** | Time between form submission and first human contact | Goal: under 5 minutes; automate immediate response |
| 15 | **Nurture sequence completion** | What % of leads complete the full nurture sequence? | Shorten sequences; improve relevance; add re-engagement triggers |

---

#### Phase 4: Bottom-of-Funnel Review (Thursday -- 20 minutes)

| # | Check Item | What to Look For | Fix |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| 16 | **Sales page / checkout conversion rate** | Benchmark: 5-10% for warm traffic; 1-5% for cold | Test pricing, testimonials, guarantees, urgency elements |
| 17 | **Application completion rate** | If using application funnel: are people finishing the application? | Shorten application; reduce friction; add progress indicators |
| 18 | **Call booking rate** | % of qualified leads who book a strategy/discovery call | Improve calendar page; add urgency; simplify booking process |
| 19 | **Call show-up rate** | Benchmark: 60-80% | Add SMS reminders; send pre-call video; confirm 24h + 1h before |
| 20 | **Close rate on calls** | Benchmark: 20-40% depending on offer price | Improve sales script; pre-qualify harder; address objections earlier |
| 21 | **Cart abandonment rate** | % who start checkout but don't complete | Add abandonment email sequence; simplify checkout; offer payment plans |

---

#### Phase 5: Performance Summary & Action Items (Friday -- 15 minutes)

| # | Check Item | Action |
|---|-----------|--------|
| 22 | **Calculate weekly KPIs** | Total leads, total sales, revenue, CAC, ROAS, conversion rates per stage |
| 23 | **Identify the biggest bottleneck** | Find the stage with the steepest drop-off -- this is your #1 priority |
| 24 | **Set one improvement goal** | Commit to ONE A/B test or optimization for the following week |
| 25 | **Document findings** | Log all metrics in a tracking spreadsheet for trend analysis |
| 26 | **Review attribution data** | Check which channels are contributing to conversions across the full journey |

### The "Fix Order" Rule

When multiple issues are found, **fix them in this order** (highest impact first):

1. **Broken tracking** -- You cannot optimize what you cannot measure
2. **Bottom-of-funnel leaks** -- These are closest to revenue (highest ROI fixes)
3. **Middle-of-funnel stalls** -- Nurture sequence and qualification issues
4. **Top-of-funnel volume** -- Traffic and lead generation problems
5. **Strategic alignment** -- Offer-market fit, messaging, and positioning

This order is counterintuitive but critical. Most businesses pour money into getting more traffic (top-of-funnel) when their real problem is a leaky bottom of funnel. Fix the bucket before you pour in more water.
